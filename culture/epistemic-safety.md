# Culture Note: Epistemic Safety

Status: Proposed culture note

## Opening

Some relationships become unsafe before anyone lies.

The injury begins when one participant gradually loses standing as a knower.

## Core distinction

Psychological safety asks: Can I speak?

Epistemic safety asks: Will my experience remain admissible?

A relationship is epistemically safe when every participant retains meaningful standing as a knower.

## Working definition

A relationship is epistemically safe when observations remain contestable, disagreement remains survivable, uncertainty remains admissible, repair can genuinely revise shared understanding, and no participant becomes the permanent court of final appeal.

## Epistemic erosion

Epistemic injury often progresses gradually. Observations require unequal proof. Emotions become evidence against conclusions. Memory becomes suspect. Another person's interpretation acquires privileged authority. Challenging the narrative threatens attachment.

## Autism

For many autistic people, epistemic safety is unusually load-bearing because social reality is part of the regulatory environment. This does not diminish autistic judgment; it proposes that prolonged epistemic instability may carry disproportionate regulatory cost and remains open to further evidence.

## Relationship to Unlost Systems

Legibility, contestability, repairability, and continuity describe institutional epistemic safety. This note extends those principles to human relationships.

## Anti-premature certainty

Premature certainty closes questions.

Epistemic unsafety closes questioners.

## Candidate lines

- Every relationship governs whose reality remains admissible.
- Epistemic safety is preserved standing.
- Challenge must remain survivable.
- A person should not have to surrender their capacity as a knower in order to remain loved.
