## Name and Spine

**Unlost Systems** is the face.

**Legible, Contestable, Repairable** is the spine.

The name is evocative. The spine is operational. The framework should not ask the name to carry precision that belongs in the discipline.

---

## Core Claim

Unlost Systems builds legible, contestable, repairable systems for complex lives.

It is for people whose complexity exceeds the tolerance of the systems they depend on.

---

## C's Load-Bearing Questions

A serious framework must answer:

1. Who is lost?
2. Lost to what?
3. By what mechanism?

These questions keep the name from becoming only a feeling.

---

## Working Answers

### Who is lost?

People whose complexity exceeds the tolerance of the systems they depend on.

This includes people whose needs are cognitive, bodily, social, bureaucratic, medical, technical, economic, or relational in ways existing processes fail to hold.

### Lost to what?

People are lost to systems that are opaque, inaccessible, brittle, punitive, fragmented, context-destroying, or indifferent to repair.

### By what mechanism?

People are lost when systems:

- make consequential decisions that cannot be understood;
- deny or obstruct challenge;
- punish executive-function load;
- fragment context across handoffs;
- treat accommodations as exceptions rather than design inputs;
- privilege institutional convenience over lived complexity;
- erase provenance;
- make repair difficult, humiliating, or impossible.

---

## Minimum Viable Discipline

### 1. Legibility

People can understand the system that affects them.

A system is not legible merely because its operators understand it. It must be explainable to the people who bear its consequences.

### 2. Contestability

People can challenge consequential decisions, classifications, workflows, and system behavior.

A process that cannot be challenged cannot legitimately claim to be fair.

### 3. Repairability

Systems preserve enough record, responsibility, and procedure to correct harm.

Repair must be a designed capability, not an heroic exception.

### 4. Complexity Tolerance

Systems do not treat complex lives as edge cases to discard.

A system that only works for simple cases is not humane enough for consequential work.

### 5. Continuity

Context survives across sessions, services, decisions, and handoffs.

A person should not have to re-prove their reality every time the system forgets.

---

## Current Planes

### CDP — Decision / Governance Plane

Decisions earn authority through governed formation, challenge, testing, adjudication, record, execution, learning, and repair.

### TinyDoor — Access / Care Plane

People are supported through humane, legible, non-punitive interfaces for complex lives.

Strong public-health claims require evidence before canonization.

### ConstantC — Continuity / Collaboration Plane

Human-AI work becomes durable through protocol, provenance, privacy, contestability, repair, and human adjudication.

---

## Guardrails

Do not let the name become a grandiosity engine.

Do not let the spine become sterile.

Do not canonize biomedical or public-health claims without evidence.

Do not claim a system is Unlost because it sounds compassionate. It must be legible, contestable, repairable, complexity-tolerant, and continuity-preserving in practice.

---

## Test

A system earns the Unlost name only if it can answer:

- What does this system make legible?
- How can affected people challenge it?
- How does repair happen when it fails?
- Whose complexity does it tolerate?
- What context survives the handoff?

---

## Candidate Manifest Sentence

Unlost Systems builds legible, contestable, repairable systems for the people existing processes were designed to lose.
