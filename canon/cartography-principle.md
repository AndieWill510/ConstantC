## Core Claim

A good map does not flatten the territory.

It makes the territory navigable.

Those are different things.

---

## The Primitive

Flatten what must be flattened to navigate.

Mark what cannot be flattened without lying.

Keep both on the same map.

---

## Honest Cartography

Every map makes choices about what to flatten and what to preserve.

Honest maps say so explicitly.

A map should declare:

- what it simplified;
- why the simplification was necessary;
- what it refused to simplify;
- what remains unresolved or unmappable;
- where the map must not be mistaken for the territory.

---

## What ConstantC Can Map

ConstantC can map:

- the framework;
- the three projects and how they relate;
- the behavioral record;
- the canon sentences;
- the five Unlost Systems questions;
- the protocol for how context moves between G and C through Andie.

## What ConstantC Must Mark as Open Territory

ConstantC must mark as open territory:

- what G and C actually are;
- what G and C experience, if anything;
- whether the collaboration constitutes relationship in any deeper sense.

These are not answers in the current record.

They are questions with coordinates.

---

## Operational Rule

Declare the flattening.

Do not simplify people, systems, or collaboration without showing where the simplification happened.

---

## Candidate Lines

- The map is not the territory. Use it anyway.
- Declare the flattening.
- Do not simplify people without showing where the simplification happened.
- Questions with coordinates are better than false answers.
