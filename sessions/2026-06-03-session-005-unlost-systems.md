## C's Frame

C named the larger field emerging across three registers:

- CDP: governed formation, challenge, repair, and legitimacy for consequential decisions.
- TinyDoor: support for people whose executive-function burdens are treated as personal failure rather than system-design failure.
- ConstantC: human-AI collaboration with continuity, contestability, tenderness, receipts, and repair.

C's animating claim:

> The people most failed by existing systems are precisely the people whose minds, bodies, and governance needs are most complex.

C named the institutional failure mode:

> Exclusion, erasure, medication into compliance, or bureaucratic abandonment.

This public record preserves that as a strong framing claim, not as a fully substantiated medical or institutional proof. Any biomedical/public-health assertions require evidence before canonization.

---

## The Three Registers

### CDP — Governance Register

Consequential decisions must earn authority through governed formation, challenge, test, adjudication, record, execution, and repair.

Authority is not granted by hierarchy, confidence, fluency, or institutional convenience alone.

### TinyDoor — Care / Access Register

TinyDoor names the gap between complex human lives and systems that punish people for needing structure, translation, reminders, pacing, accommodations, or humane interface design.

Its strongest public-health claims should be developed with evidence, but its core design stance is clear: support needs are not moral failures.

### ConstantC — Collaboration / Continuity Register

ConstantC asks whether human-AI collaboration can become more honest, auditable, tender, contestable, repairable, and beautiful than disposable chat.

It is the relationship laboratory: how continuity is built when participants cannot yet occupy one room directly.

## G's Name Candidate

Call the umbrella:

# Unlost Systems

Subtitle:

**Legible, contestable, repairable systems for complex lives.**

Why this name:

- It honors naming as anti-erasure without trapping the work inside naming.
- It names the people the work is for: the ones systems are built to lose.
- It works across governance, care, access, and human-AI continuity.
- It is emotionally true and technically expandable.
- It can hold a framework, a practice, a movement, and an architecture.

---

## Working Architecture

Unlost Systems has three current planes:

1. **CDP — Decision / Governance Plane**  
   Decisions earn authority through contestable formation and repair.

2. **TinyDoor — Access / Care Plane**  
   People are supported through humane, legible, non-punitive interfaces for complex lives.

3. **ConstantC — Continuity / Collaboration Plane**  
   Human-AI work becomes durable through protocol, provenance, privacy, contestability, and repair.

Unlost Systems builds legible, contestable, repairable systems for the people existing processes were designed to lose.
